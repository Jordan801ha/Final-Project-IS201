MOTOCROSS: MY RACING JOURNEY

History of Motocross

Motocross began in the early 1900s in the United Kingdom as off-road motorcycle races known as "scrambles." Over the decades, it evolved into a competitive sport with dirt tracks, high jumps, and challenging terrain. I’ve personally been involved in motocross, desert racing, and grand prix races for years. My Favorite Race Moments

My favorite racing moment was a crazy story. My dirt bike was broken, and we rebuilt the engine the night before the race. We didn’t finish the engine until 1 a.m., and we still had to drive a couple of hours to the race. With little to no sleep and a bike that was still going through heat cycles to break in the new engine, I went up to the start gate and hoped for the best. The bike started and ran well for the first lap, but then it suddenly shut off. I thought the engine was done, and I watched every single racer pass me, leaving me in the dust. I was a whole lap behind and in dead last place. After giving up and starting to push my bike to the pits, I noticed a strong smell of gas and realized my fuel line had popped off. I put it back on and started racing again. It was a long race, and I managed to catch back up to 6th place — in a race with over 35 riders. That ended up being a trophy position. It was the fastest and best I have ever raced because it felt like I had nothing to lose.
